Pete Sampras | Net Worth

During his career, Sampras earned $43 million, equivalent to about $75 million.

Pete Sampras | Charity Works

As a Tim & Tom Gullikson Foundation board member, Sampras hosted “Pete Sampras Classic” in March 2000.

The previous coach of Sampras, Tim, had an unfortunate death due to brain cancer in 1996. Thus, his family established the foundation to assist patients and their families in coping with the illness.

The “Pete Sampras Classic” event attracted about thirty Hollywood, sports, and media celebrities and was able to raise over US$ 90,000 for the benefit of the foundation.

In 1997, Pete started the Aces for Charity. For this, he would donate 100 US dollars for every ace he served. Similarly, he doubled the amount in 1999.

Due to his contribution and the support of many sponsors, he was able to raise about US$ 1 million in three years.

Likewise, in 2002 the Sampras couples joined together with different celebrities in an ad campaign conducted by Ford Motors for breast cancer awareness.

Kate Spade provided the participants in the awareness program with a limited edition red scarf.

The proceeds from the sale of the scarfs were donated to the Susan G.Komen Breast Cancer Foundation.

Endorsements

Due to his outstanding performances, Sampras was given many endorsement partnerships.

One of his well-known partnerships is with Wilson. He was constant in using the Wilson Pro Staff Original throughout his entire career. His excellent serve helped Wilson to sell a lot of tennis racquets.

After spending the initial year of his career with Sergio Tacchini, in 1994, Sampras signed a deal with Nike’s renowned sports brand. His contract with Nike included the shoes and additional carry bag too.

Along with clothing and rackets endorsements, he was also offered plenty of deals to help him raise his wealth.

He had sponsorship deals with Dannon and Pizza Hut, essentially playing off his reputation as a relaxed and calm tennis player.

Books

The former tennis player has two compelling books about his journey.

Facing Sampras: Symposium of a Champion was written by Scoop Malinowski, and the second book, Pete Sampras: A Champion’s Mind, was the autobiography of Sampras.

Scoop Malinowski wrote the book to recollect what it was like to play against Pete Sampras.

However, the book which captured everyone’s attention was the autobiography.

Because of Sampras’s secretive nature, this created curiosity among the people about the book’s release.

Pete Sampras | Career

On August 12, 1971, Sampras was born in Washington DC, United States, to Soterios and Georgia Sampras.

At the age of 16, Pete Sampras was ready to go professional, and within a year, he moved up from 893 in the world rankings to 97. In the same year, he had defeated several top-ranked tennis players.

Sampras won a Grand Slam single at the French Open, followed by his first major win at the US Open by defeating Andre Agassi to become the youngest sing champion.

In 1991, Pete Sampras won his first-ever career tiles at the Tennis Masters Cup following the victory over the Davis Cup.

After gaining the top spot, he also won his first title at Wimbledon and won the US Open.

He won another Australian Open and Wimbledon title in 1994 and 1997. Again, in 1998, he won another Wimbledon title.

Although Sampras lost his title to Andre Agassi in 1998, he later in 2000 returned to his top spot but again failed to Agassi in the Australian Open.

In 2001, Sampra slowly began to dip with no victory over any titles. For the first time in his 12 years of career, and he had failed to win anything. This led him to tenth place in the world ranking.

Thus, after defeating his old opponent, Andre Agassi, and winning his last US Open title in 2002, he retired from his tennis career.
