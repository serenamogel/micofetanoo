Park Seo Joon Started His Acting Career in 2011

As we all know, Park Seo Joon made his entertainment debut in 2011 from Bang Yong-guk‘s music video “I Remember.” After that, Park appeared in Dream High 2, followed by Pots of Gold, One Warm Word, and A Witch’s Love. And then, from October 2013 to April 2015, Seo Joon became the host of Music Bank.

But with time, he had a breakthrough from the drama, Kill Me, Heal Me, and She Was Pretty. Park reportedly made around $25,000 per episode of ‘She Was Pretty.’ Similarly, his fame skyrocketed after starring in the 2016 historical drama, Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth alongside Go Ara, Park Hyung Sik, and other prominent names.

Likewise, Park got the title of “Master of Romantic Comedy” from the 2017 romance drama, Fight for My Way, opposite Kim Ji-won. The show was a major hit and topped ratings in South Korea. That same year, Park made his silver screen debut as a lead role in an action-comedy titled Midnight Runners alongside Kang Ha-neul.

Moreover, Seo Joon won the Best New Actor award at major film award ceremonies, including the Grand Bell Awards and Korean Association of Film Critics Awards. As if he was not successful already, Seo Joon’s fame escalated to a new height after the success of tvN’s drama, What’s Wrong with Secretary Kim. Not to mention, the two leads, Seo Joon and Park Min-young were even rumored to be secretly dating in real life.

As for his recent work, Park starred in JTBC’s drama Itaewon Class based on a famous webtoon of the same name. The drama received a lot of positive feedback for its interesting plots and realistic lives surrounding people in the Itaewon area.
