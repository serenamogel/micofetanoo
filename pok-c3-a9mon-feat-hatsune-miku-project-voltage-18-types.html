Pokémon feat. Hatsune Miku Project VOLTAGE 18 Types/Songs (Japanese: ポケモン feat. 初音ミク Project VOLTAGE 18 Types/Songs), abbreviated as PokéMiku (Japanese: ポケミク) is a music project created as a collaboration between Pokémon and the Vocaloid character Hatsune Miku. It consists of 18 original songs and illustrations featuring Hatsune Miku.

Songs

Beginning on September 29, 2023, 18 original songs and music videos by 18 different Vocaloid producers were released on the respective producer's YouTube channel, with 18 being chosen due to it being the number of Pokémon types. The songs featured Hatsune Miku and other Piapro Characters, and most sampled background music and sound effects from the core series Pokémon games. The scope of the project later expanded to include 20 different Vocaloid producers. The first four participating producers were announced on September 28.[1] The next four producers were announced on November 24, 2023.[2] The next five were announced on January 26, 2024.[3] The next four producers was announced on February 22, 2024.[4] The final producer was not announced in advance but revealed directly with the release of the song.[5][6]

Additional songs

After the release of the 18th original song, it was announced that additional songs would be produced,[7] with the next producer announced the same day, on March 9, 2024.[8] The next producer was announced after the release of the 19th original song on May 10, 2024,[9] along with the release date of a compilation CD.[10]

The illustration part of the collaboration consisted of six artists making 18 illustrations based on the theme of "What if Hatsune Miku was a XX-type Pokémon Trainer?", showing Miku with 18 different partner Pokémon. The pieces were released every weekday (excluding holidays) at 7:00 PM JST from September 4 to 28, 2023, prior to the release of the first song. Each entry in the Pokémon Trainer series is appended with a Pokédex entry of the Pokémon paired with Miku, serving as a hint on why the Pokémon was chosen to pair with Miku.

Comics

In the January 2024 issue of Famitsu that focused on Project VOLTAGE, Kino Takahashi[57][58] drew two four-panel comics focusing on the Fairy, Psychic, Normal and Fighting-type Miku Pokémon Trainer designs.

Trivia

  • This collaboration between Pokémon and Hatsune Miku was announced on her birthday (August 31) and her 16th anniversary (her age).[59]
  • Hatsune Miku had a previous collaboration with Pokémon in 2020. Snow Miku x Alolan Vulpix was a collaboration with Pokémon Local Acts as a partnership of Hokkaido's Sapporo Snow Festival.[60]
  • Each Pokémon that is paired with Hatsune Miku has themes associated with Miku, namely music, singing, software (in Rotom and Miraidon's case), or leeks (in Sirfetch'd's case). Miraidon may additionally be a reference to the Hatsune Miku: Project Mirai series of rhythm games, as well as the meaning of Hatsune Miku's name, "the first sound from the future".[61]
