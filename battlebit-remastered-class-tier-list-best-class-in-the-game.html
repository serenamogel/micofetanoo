BattleBit Remastered Class Tier List: Best Class in the Game

January 2024 · 4 minute read

BattleBit Remastered has 6 distinct classes for players to pick, and get familiar with. Squad Leader, Assault, Medic, Engineer, Support, and Recon.

In this tier list, I will showcase the best class in the game, in BattleBit Remastered. Here is a quick overview of the tier list.

I will also go in-depth as to what makes the S-Tier classes so good, and why they are worth their spot. Especially for those of us who try out games like these through peer pressure.

Though always keep in mind, that these classes are meant to be fun, and if you like one that may be listed lower, that doesn’t mean you should never use it.

If you want to know more about the game, check out our guide on how to customize characters, in BattleBit Remastered.

BattleBit Remastered: S-Tier CLasses

S-Tier characters are the best of the best, either through utility, damage, or squad value.

The Medic class is responsible for everyone’s health around them, though the Medic is still equipped for war with either SMGs or PDWs.

Medics by default have noticeable advantages with bandages. They apply them twice as fast to both bleeding and downed teammates as well as themselves, and they can carry 20 of them instead of the usual 4.

They can be distributed to teammates by dropping the bandages, with the X button.

Medics equip standard armor and can use light, ranger, or heavy armor as alternative options. They can also use normal or heavy backpacks.

The Medic has access to the same gadgets as all other classes, but they uniquely only have a single secondary gadget, the Medic Kit. It can be used infinitely by holding the left or right mouse buttons to heal yourself or a nearby ally.

The Medic Kit can also be dropped, and used by allies without the fear of losing it after.

The Engineer’s job is all about vehicles, both destroying them and repairing them. 

They have exclusive access to RPGs, Anti-Vehicle Grenades, and the Repair Tool. They also have the widest range of weapons to choose from, only lacking heavy machine guns and sniper rifles.

Rockets are also obviously very good at knocking down walls enemies are using for cover. Considering how prevalent BattleBit Remastered’s tanks, helicopters, and boats are, the Engineer class is relevant across all kinds of encounters.

Their Repair Tool can be swapped out for any explosive primary gadget, and the RPG can use fragmentation rockets instead of H.E.A.T. to become even deadlier to infantry. 

Engineers wear standard armor, and they have access to light and heavy armor. They also have the unique trait of wearing no armor at all. As a reward for not wearing armor, Engineers gain a massive increase in aiming speed.

They can use light, normal, or heavy backpacks.

Final Thoughts

In a multiplayer team-based game, filling a slot in your team can make all the difference to what is needed.

The S-Tier class discussed are a way for both experienced players and players that have trouble with games like this, to help out the team without having to carry the damage of it all.

Healing can be a sustainable way to provide help, and so is either repairing or destroying vehicles, when the going gets tough.

If you are planning to check out the Medic then, check out our guide on how to heal, in BattleBit Remastered. Knowing how to effectively fill the class’s shoes, will make your starting games easier to master.

Now that you know the best class in BattleBit Remastered, do you feel more prepared to tackle the game with friends? Don’t feel pressured to only use the S-Tier classes though.

Enjoy the game for what it has to offer, and let us all know in the comments down below what class is your favorite, and which one you feel is more beginner friendly.
